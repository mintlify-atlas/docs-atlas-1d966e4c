## Uploading a recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the upload form">
    From your dashboard, click **+ Nueva Receta**. The recipe form opens as a modal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the metadata">
    Enter the public-facing details for your recipe:

    - **Título** — the recipe title
    - **Subtítulo** — a short tagline
    - **Descripción Pública** — a brief description visible before subscribing
    - **Tiempo de preparación** — estimated prep time (e.g., `45m`)
    - **Dificultad** — select Fácil, Media, or Difícil
    - **Porciones** — number of servings
    - **Categoría** — select from the available [cooking categories](#cooking-categories)

    <Note>
      Metadata fields are stored as plaintext. They are visible to all users browsing the vault, including non-subscribers.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the encrypted content">
    In the **Contenido Cifrado** section, fill in the details that only your subscribers will be able to read:

    - **Ingredientes** — add each ingredient with a name and quantity. Click **+ Añadir** to add more rows.
    - **Preparación** — add each step as a numbered instruction. Click **+ Añadir Paso** to add more.
    - **Galería de Imágenes** — add image URLs for each photo in your recipe. Click **+ Añadir URL** to add more. A thumbnail preview appears as you type.

    At least one complete ingredient, one preparation step, and one image URL are required before you can submit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the recipe">
    Click **Cifrar y Publicar**. The platform:

    1. Encrypts the ingredient list, preparation steps, and images using **AES-128-GCM** with a freshly generated random key.
    2. Saves the encrypted content as a `.enc` file in the external vault.
    3. Records the recipe metadata and a **SHA-256 integrity hash** of the ciphertext in the database.
    4. Stores the AES key in the `clave_receta` table, associated with your chef account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the upload">
    The modal closes and the recipe appears in your dashboard catalog. Subscribers with an active contract can now access the encrypted content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Editing a recipe

- Click **Editar** on any recipe card in your dashboard.
- The platform fetches the `.enc` file from the vault, decrypts it using the stored AES key, and pre-fills the recipe form with your existing content.
- Make any changes to the metadata or encrypted content fields.
- Click **Re-cifrar y Actualizar** to save.

<Note>
When you save an edited recipe, the platform generates a **new AES key**, re-encrypts the content, writes a new `.enc` file to the vault, and deletes the old encrypted file. All metadata, the new encrypted file reference, and the new key are updated atomically in the database.
</Note>

---

## Deleting a recipe

- Click **Borrar** on any recipe card in your dashboard.
- Confirm the deletion in the prompt that appears.

<Warning>
Deletion is permanent. Both the database record and the `.enc` file in the external vault are removed immediately and **cannot be recovered**.
</Warning>

## Recipe content structure

Understanding what is encrypted and what is not helps you decide what to put where:

| Field | Storage | Visible to |
|---|---|---|
| Title | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Subtitle | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Description | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Prep time | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Difficulty | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Servings | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Category | Plaintext (database) | Everyone |
| Ingredients | Encrypted (`.enc` file) | Active subscribers only |
| Preparation steps | Encrypted (`.enc` file) | Active subscribers only |
| Images | Encrypted (`.enc` file) | Active subscribers only |
